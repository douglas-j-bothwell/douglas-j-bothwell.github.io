Costs in the LOP (Lower-Order SONET/SDH) Layer

The direct LOP route cost consists of an LOP TM tributary port cost at both source and destination node. It depends on the bit rate of the LOP connection.

The indirect LOP route cost consists of the cost of each SONET/SDH trunk along the route (see Costs in the DCL (Digital Client or Higher-Order SONET/SDH) Layer). These SONET trunk costs are divided by the number of LOP connections that each trunk can host. For example, one STM-1 SDH trunk can accommodate 21 LOP connections at a VC-2 rate.
