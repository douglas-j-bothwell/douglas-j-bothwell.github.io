Business Group Topology
The Business Group Topology window allows you to visualize data exchanges between Business Groups (Business Group containers are, of course, Business Groups that contain other Business Groups). The topology view identifies Business Groups that communicate with each other and illustrates the nature of that communication in the form of performance and utilization metrics.
To invoke the Business Group Topology view, select View > Topology > BG Topology.
Figure 1
BG Topology View
 
Business Group Topology Window
The BG Topology window has two panes: the selector pane (left) and the display pane (right). The selector pane is used to choose the groups for which topology information is shown in the display pane.
There is also an overview pane which is launched by clicking the arrow in the upper left-hand corner of the display pane. The Overview Pane is a navigational aid.
To view a topology, select one or more groups in the selector pane (use ctrl+click or shift+click to select multiple groups). Then click Go to display them in the display pane.
To open a new window, repeat the View > Topology > BG Topology sequence.
To clear the display and begin again, do one of the following
Select a new Business Group and click Go (the display pane automatically refreshes).
Click the Clear Graph button on the toolbar (see Business Group Topology Toolbar for more information).
If connected to an appliance, two tree-selectors are displayed: Business Group Containers, a hierarchical view, and All Business Groups, a flat view. The hierarchical view allows you to choose Business Groups by navigating the group hierarchy. The flat view allows you to easily find groups if you already know the group name. On the director, only the All Business Groups list is presented.
If connected to a director, rather than a regular appliance, the topology window includes an appliance selector. All data displayed in the topology window is retrieved from the selected appliance.
Examining group topology on a Domain Director yields different results than using an appliance that is part of a domain. If Business Groups have been configured to aggregate data back to the director (see Aggregating Business Group Data), then the group topology tool run from the director can display data collected across all machines in the cluster. However, if the group topology tool fetches data from an appliance, it is only able to present data that was collected by that appliance.
However, the group hierarchy is flat on the director. At present, the group topology tool must fetch data from an appliance to display anything other than a flat group topology. (For more information, see Hierarchy Through Business Group Containers).

The Go button produces different results for a single group selection than for a multiple group selection:
If you select a single group, then the display pane shows the selected group and any other groups that have exchanged data with the selected group (also known as connected groups), as illustrated above.
If you select multiple groups, then the display pane shows only the selected groups and the traffic between them; other groups that have exchanged data with the selected groups (connected groups) are not displayed.
An arrow connects each pair of groups that have exchanged data. By default, this arrow represents the value of the metric Throughput (Inbound and Outbound) between the groups. For other metrics, where applicable, the direction of the arrow denotes the direction of the metric (Inbound / Outbound) for Utilization Metrics. For TCP Connection metrics, a square denotes the TCP Server side. For TCP Turn metrics, a square denotes the Server side.
Business Group Topology Toolbar
The following controls are available at the top of the Business Group Topology window:
Figure 3
Business Group Topology - Toolbar Window
 
The following list describes the functions of the controls on the top from left to right:
Clear Graph - Clears the current graph from the display pane.
Selection Mode - Select a node in the graph. The selected node is subject to the next operation.
Zoom mode - Click and sweep on areas to zoom in on.
Fit Content - Adjusts the zoom level so that the graph fits inside the display pane.
Zoom To 100% - Shows the graph to scale.
Zoom In x2 - Doubles the zoom level.
Zoom Out x2 - Halves the zoom level.
Drill Up - undoes the last drill-down operation and returns to the previous topology view.
Show Metric Labels - Shows the selected metric between connected groups. These labels can be turned off using the Show Metric Label control in the Business Group Topology Context Menu.
Show Alerts - Shades the group nodes in the graph by the severity of the last static or adaptive alert generated for that group during the current time selection. For more information, see Alerts.
Select Background Image - Sets background image in the display pane. The image can be removed by right-clicking in an empty part of the display pane and selecting Clear Background from the menu.
Delete - Deletes selected groups from the graph.
Snapshot - Exports the current graph to a .gif, .jpg or .png file. You can also copy the graph to the clipboard (choose File > Snapshot) and then paste it into a graphics program such as Paint.
Help Pages - Shows the Operations Manual help pages.
Layout - Select the layout view of the graph in the display pane. There are five to choose from: Tree (Horizontal), Tree (Vertical), Organic, Circular and Orthogonal.
The following list describes the functions of the controls on the bottom from left to right:
Top - Shows the number of connected groups displayed in the graph.
By - Selects the metrics shown in the graph.
Metric unit menu - Selects the metric units shown in the graph.
Toolbar Options - Choose which controls you’d like in the toolbar.
Note: You must press Enter after changing the number in the Top field for your choice to take effect.

Drill-Down Controls
The drill-down controls are only available for Business Group Containers (see also Hierarchy Through Business Group Containers). Drill-downs can be reversed by the Drill-Up control. Note that in the following discussion, “group” refers to both Business Groups and Business Group Containers.
Drill-downs help you visualize connectivity details for Business Group containers.
Drill-downs visualize the first-level sub-groups of a Business Group container and all the Business Groups or Business Group containers each sub-group is connected to.
Drill-downs show only the first-level sub-groups of Business Group containers. That is, if a container has containers as sub-groups, the drill-down shows only the sub-containers, not their sub-groups.
All drill-down operations may be undone by right-clicking in the main topology window outside of the Business Group container, or by selecting the Drill-Up control in the toolbar.
When you select Drill-Down on a Business Group container or double-click on a node, AppResponse does the following:
Expands the selected container to show the first-level sub-groups for which AppResponse observed traffic during the current project time. The sub-groups appear in a yellow box that includes the name of the container. Arrows represent traffic between internal groups.
Shows traffic between these sub-groups and the container’s connected groups (both Business Group and Business Group containers).
The connected groups are not expanded, nor is their traffic with groups other than the selected Business Group container discovered.
This might be represented, using the same convention as group paths, as Business Group Container > First-Level Sub-Groups > Connected Groups. (Group paths are described in Drill-Downs and Metric Availability.)
With Drill-Down you can see the specific relationships between each sub-group and the container’s connected groups, but no further connectivity, answering the question:
“What Business Groups or Business Group containers are each sub-group of the selected container talking to?”
