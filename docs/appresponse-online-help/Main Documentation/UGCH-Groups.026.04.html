Group Drill-Down
Groups provide a simple means to monitor the aggregate behavior of a set of IP addresses. However, in many cases it is necessary to observe the behavior of a subset of traffic, called a drill-down, within a group. For instance, assume you have defined a Business Group to represent the aggregate behavior of a set of web servers. Group drill-down is used to examine individual web servers within the group.
To drill down into a specific group, click the “+” button to the left of the group name.

Drill-Downs and Metric Availability
Each group provides a different set of drill-downs against all or some of the traffic seen by the appliance. The Total Traffic, IP Protocol, Application, IP Address, IP Conversation, and Prefix/24 Groups offer drill-downs into all traffic seen by the appliance. The Dest AS, ISP AS, Business Group, and Business Group Link Groups offer drill-downs into the corresponding subset of the traffic seen by the appliance. In addition, only a subset of metric types are available in the ISP AS group (see ISP AS Drill-Downs).
To understand drill-downs, it is important to keep in mind the distinction between defined, undefined and other applications.
An “other application” is an application that is not defined. It could either be a simple protocol/port combination or a recognized application.
A “defined application” is simply one defined using the Desktop Console. A defined application may also be based on a recognized application.
For more information, see Applications.
The following tables list the types of available drill-downs. The drill-down from a group into a subset of its traffic is indicated by the “>” symbol; a particular sequence of drill-downs is called a Group Path. The group path shown in Figure 2 is “Total Traffic > TCP > Applications”.

Applications Drill-Downs
The Applications Group displays all protocols and applications without separating them by category as does the Total Traffic Group: any TCP, any UDP, and any non-UPD-or-TCP Protocol. The Applications Group offers an easy way to discover the top N applications running on your network without requiring drill-down into the separate TCP, UDP, and non-UDP-or-TCP protocol categories of the Total Traffic Group.
