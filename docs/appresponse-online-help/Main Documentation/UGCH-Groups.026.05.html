Business Group Manager
The Business Group Manager is used to create, edit or delete Business Groups and Business Group containers. To access the Business Group Manager, choose Tools > Business Group Manager, then select Business Group.
Figure 13
Business Group Manager
 
The Business Group Manager displays a list of existing Business Groups. There are no Business Groups defined on a freshly-installed appliance.
If your appliance belongs to a domain, you should also review the following sections after reading this section:
Distributed Business Groups
Aggregating Business Group Data
Creating a Business Group
A Business Group does not appear if the selected time window is before the creation time for that group. It may take several minutes before data for a newly-created Business Group can be viewed.
Procedure 1
Defining a New Business Group
1.
Starting on the Identity tab (the default tab), click New in the tool bar on the lower left of the Business Group Manager window. The new Business Group is displayed and selected in the table at the top of the window. The group attributes may be edited in the lower pane of the window.
2.
Click in the Name field and enter a unique name to identify the Business Group (required). The name can include any character except for the following:
• 
comma (,)
• 
forward-slash (/)
• 
close-triangle-bracket (>)
3.
Click in the Description field and enter a description of the Business Group (optional).
4.
Leave the Classification Field as ‘Business Group’ (the default value)
5.
Leave the Status of the group as Active. For information on Business Group status, see Group Status.
Figure 14
Business Group Manager - Definition Tab
 
6.
Click in the Group Data field to enter the list of IP addresses or Autonomous System (AS) numbers for the group. IP addresses and AS numbers should be separated by commas.
Errors in the list of IP/AS members are indicated by a red font. To view the error list click the information icon (see below) to launch the error viewer.
Figure 15
Information Icon
 
Valid formats for a member are as follows:
• 
IP Address: #.#.#.#
• 
AS Number: #
• 
IP Address Prefix Form: #.#.#.0/24
• 
IP Address Range: #.#.#.# - #.#.#.# (where the first IP is smaller or equal to the second)
• 
AS Address Range: # - # (where the first AS is smaller or equal to the second)
The Business Group Manager provides the ability to make multiple changes to the client group list and save them to the database all at once. These changes may include creating, deleting, and editing multiple client groups.

These changes are not saved until APPLY is clicked. Pressing CANCEL erases all changes entered since the last APPLY and closes the Business Group Manager.
WARNING: To avoid excessive load on the appliance, minimize the number of comma-separated items in the Business Group Manager > Business Group Definition Tab > IP/AS Members field
When the appliance calculates metrics in real time, it checks monitored packets against each comma-separated item (IP, IP range, or AS) in each Business Group definition. A complex BG definition, one that includes a long list of items, can increase computation loads and affect monitoring performance on the appliance.
To keep your Business Group definitions as simple as possible, it is good practice to
• 
Include no more than 20 comma-separated entries in any Business Group definition.
• 
Specify IP address ranges, rather than individual IPs, whenever possible.
7.
(Optional) The Utilization Bandwidth tab has fields that specify utilization bandwidths for the group. The primary use case for this feature is to monitor utilization at remote offices with limited bandwidths. For example, you can create Static-Any alerts or SLA Dashboards that show violations when utilization for a Business Group exceeds a certain threshold such as 95%. The bandwidth is the speed of the WAN link used by the remote office.
Figure 16
Business Group Manager - Utilization Bandwidth Tab
 
By default, the Inbound and Outbound fields are set to 0 and the appliance measures 0% for the Utilization (Inbound) [%] and Utilization (Outbound) [%] metrics. As soon as you set these fields to positive values and save the settings, the appliance collects utilization percentages for the Business Group.
8.
Click OK or Apply to save changes, or CANCEL to abort.
End of Procedure 1
 
 
The Business Group Manager includes a Distribution tab if it is run on the Domain Director (see Domain Director). This tab is used to specify a list of appliances for the purpose of distributing configuration information (see Distributing Configuration Information).
Business group definitions can be imported and exported in CSV format. For more details, see Importing and Exporting Configuration Data.

Viewing the Business Group Container Hierarchy
Business group containers are used to defined hierarchy among Business Groups. The Business Group Manager includes a Container Tree tab to help visualize this hierarchy.
Figure 18
Business Group Manager - Container Tree Tab
 
The Business Group Manager assembles a hierarchical Business Group tree based on the Business Group containers that are currently defined. As a result, the container tree for a new Business Group is only available after the new group has been saved. Remember that Business Group containers can include other Business Group containers making it possible to define Business Group hierarchies with multiple levels. The Container Tree shows the hierarchical Business Group tree at two levels:
1)
All Business Group containers (fully-expanded) that directly or indirectly contain the Business Group.
2)
The current Business Group container (fully-expanded).
The Container Tree provides allows you to drill-down on any Business Group container in the hierarchy, allowing you to examine the full Business Group hierarchy.
The Container Tree provides a simple visualization of the Business Group hierarchy. The full hierarchy can be explored in much more detail using the Topology Tool.

Editing a Business Group
1.

End of Procedure 3
 
 
Deleting a Business Group
When a Business Group is deleted, all data captured for the group is removed from the appliance. The group is no longer available within the Desktop Console. The Group Status for a Business Group can be set to inactive, which stops new data collection but does not remove existing data.
Procedure 4
Deleting a Business Group
1.
In the Business Group Manager, select a Business Group in the table.
2.
Click the Delete button in the tool bar on the lower left of the Business Group Manager window.
3.
Click OK or Apply to save changes, or CANCEL to abort.
End of Procedure 4
 
 
Group Status
When a Business Group is deleted, all data captured for the group is removed from the appliance.
There are certain cases where it is desirable to retain the captured data for a Business Group that is no longer valid. For instance, a network reorganization may eliminate a local server farm. In this case, the appliance should not collect data for the old IP addresses of the server farm, but the user may wish to keep the old data collected for this Business Group. In this event, the user should change the status of the Business Group to inactive. The appliance stops capturing data for an inactive group, but existing data is retained in the database.
Figure 19
Group Status
 
In addition, IP addresses that belong only to inactive Business Groups are not considered when selecting the Top IP addresses during the aggregation process. For more information, see Group and Drill-Down Aggregation.
