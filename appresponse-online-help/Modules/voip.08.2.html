Video Monitoring Support
VoIP Monitoring Module supports monitoring for video conferencing systems and point-to-point video calls. this support does not include the following:
IP-TV
Web-based streaming protocols such as those used by Flash-based sites like youtube.com
Proprietary video programs that do not use RTP as their video-transmission mechanism, such as Skype
In Lync environments: Monitoring IM, desktop sharing, or voice/video calls with greater than 2 participants
Conferencing support is specific to traffic to and from dedicated conferencing equipment. In the context of this functionality, a conference is a set of Voice and/or Video calls to the same conference number. AppResponse treats calls in ad-hoc conferences (such as three-way calling) as simple point-to-point calls.

Important Notes
Note the following:
The appliance will not collect any video metrics if it does not see video signalling data (due to spanning, appliance placement, or encryption).
The appliance collects metrics for video streams with supported codecs only. For a complete list, see Supported Video Codecs.
If you are monitoring video traffic in an H.323 environment, you must specify the IPs of all hosts that act as Gatekeepers in the monitored environment. (Different vendors use different terms for the host(s) that play the Gatekeeper role; if in doubt, consult the vendor documentation.)
When you determine the Gatekeeper hosts, do the following:
–
Go to the VoIP web UI > Administration Tools > Protocol Settings page.
–
Under Protocol Handling Settings, select the Specify Signaling Servers checkbox, enter the IPs of the Gatekeeper hosts (comma-separated list), and click Apply at the bottom of the page.
