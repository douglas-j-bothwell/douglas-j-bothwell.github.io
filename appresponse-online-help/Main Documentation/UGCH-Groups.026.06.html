Business Group Link Manager
The Business Group link manager is used to create, edit or delete Business Group links. To begin, go to the Desktop Console> Tools > Business Group Manager, then select Business Group Link. Note—You cannot define more than 100 Business Group Links on an appliance.
Figure 20
Business Group Link Manager
 
The Business Group link manager displays a list of existing Business Group links. There are no Business Group links defined on a freshly installed appliance.
Business group links cannot be defined on the Domain Director (see Domain Director). All Business Group links are local to the appliance on which they are defined.
Creating a Business Group Link
New Business Group links can only be viewed within the Desktop Console in time selections after the group was defined. It may take several minutes before data for a newly-created Business Group link can be viewed.
Procedure 5
Creating a New Business Group Link.
1.
Click New in the tool bar on the lower left of the Business Group link manager window. The new Business Group link is displayed and selected in the table at the top of the window. The group attributes may be edited in the lower pane of the window.
2.
Click in the Name field and enter a unique name to identify the Business Group link (required).
3.
Optionally, click in the Description field and enter a description of the Business Group link.
4.
Select the first Business Group for the link in the Business Group 1 field.
5.
Select the second Business Group for the link in the Business Group 2 field.
6.
Leave the Status of the group as Active. The Business Group link status works the same way as Business Group status.
7.
Click OK or Apply to save changes, or CANCEL to abort.
End of Procedure 5
 
 
When a Business Group link is deleted, all data captured for the link is removed from the appliance. Business group links can be set to inactive, which stops new data collection, but does not remove existing data (see Group Status).
Business group link definitions can be imported and exported in CSV format. For more details, see Importing and Exporting Configuration Data.
