Aggregating Data to the Director
Data collected on each appliance can be aggregated back to the director. It is desirable to aggregate Business Group data to the director for a few reasons:
Data collected on multiple appliances can be summed on the director. This is useful when traffic for a single logical Business Group is spread across multiple appliances. This should only be done when each appliance sees unique traffic for the Business Group in question.
The director provides a single location for examining cluster-wide data if relevant Business Groups from each appliance are aggregated.
Users can connect directly to the director to examine data collected for each Business Group. This saves time since it is not necessary to connect directly to each appliance separately.
Data for the following group types can be aggregated to the director:
1)
Business Groups [implicit Director Group]
2)
Applications [implicit Director Group]
3)
Total Traffic (or any Total Traffic drill-down) [explicit Director Group]
4)
ISP AS (or any ISP AS drill-down) [explicit Director Group]
Data aggregated from an appliance to the director is said to belong to a Director Group and must be examined using a Director Group path. In the case of Business Groups (and applications), a Director Group is defined implicitly after following the procedures in Aggregating Business Group Data and Aggregating Application Data respectively. In the case of Total Traffic or ISP group path, a Director Group must be explicitly defined using the Director Group Manager. See Aggregating Total Traffic or ISP Group Path Data.
Note: If an appliance is unavailable (either due to network connectivity or the appliance being down), the director waits up to 20 minutes before it aggregates the data. If an appliance is not available within this time-out period the director aggregates the data of the available appliances and moves on. At this point the data are incomplete.
Aggregating Business Group Data
To aggregate Business Group data to the director, click the Collect on Director checkbox in the Business Group Manager Distribution tab.
Figure 15
Business Group Manager - Collect on Director
 
Each appliance with Collect on Director enabled aggregates data collected for that Business Group to the director. The data for the Business Group on the appliance represents the sum of all data collected by each appliance with Collect on Director enabled. This option should only be enabled for appliances that see unique traffic for the Business Group, otherwise some data is counted twice (or more).
Appliances with Collect on Director enabled do not aggregate 1-minute metrics to the director. As a result, only 5-minute, 1-hour and 1-day metric granularities are available for these Business Groups on the director. To examine 1-minute metrics, users must connect directly to the appliance or transparently connect to the appliance using a group table on the director.
Business group data aggregated to the director can only be examined through a Director Group (see Director Group Table) or through the group topology window (see Business Group Topology).
Aggregating Application Data
Application data collected on each appliance can be aggregated back to the director in the same way as Business Group data (as described in Aggregating Business Group Data). In fact, application data is only aggregated to the director for those Business Groups that are also being aggregated. Aggregating application data to the director is meaningless if Business Groups are not already aggregated, since applications are drill-downs within Business Groups.
To aggregate application data to the director, click the Collect on Director checkbox in the Defined Application Manager.
Note: No more than 10 applications can be aggregated to the director for Business Groups.
Figure 16
Defined Application Manager—Collect on Director
 
Application data aggregated to the director can only be examined through Director Groups. See Director Group Table.
Aggregating Total Traffic or ISP Group Path Data
The Director Group Manager is used to define explicit Director Groups for the purpose of aggregating Total Traffic or ISP group data from the appliances to the director. Director Groups can be defined for Total Traffic and ISP groups or for any of the drill-downs available from these groups. See Group Drill-Down.
Follow these steps to define a Director Group:
Procedure 7
Defining a Director Group
1.
Go to the Tools menu on a Desktop Console connected to the director and select Director Group Manager.

All HTTP Traffic across all Appliances
The following Director Group aggregates all HTTP traffic seen by all appliances in a domain to the Director. Assume that the domain consists of an director named cuda0 and two appliances named cuda1 and cuda2.
 
Field
Value
Director Group Type
Total Traffic
Members
cuda1:Total Traffic >TCP >Applications > HTTP
cuda2:Total Traffic >TCP >Applications > HTTP
All HTTP traffic captured by cuda1 and cuda2 is aggregated to the Director (cuda0) and made available in the new Director Group. The traffic captured by cuda1 and cuda2 is summed together in the Director Group. The Director Group can be examined in a Director Group Table.
Similarly, a Director Group can be used to sum the total utilization of protocols that include both TCP and UDP ports. For instance, Real Audio and Video often uses TCP ports 554 and 7070 as well as UDP ports 6970 to 7170. A Director Group is required to sum the utilization across all these ports.
Incomplete Aggregation
If aggregation is incomplete due to an appliance being unreachable, the director automatically recollects and aggregates the data from this appliance up to the last 6 hours from the time the appliance becomes available again. This process is called Online Data Recovery to distinguish it from Offline Data Recovery, which is user-driven.
